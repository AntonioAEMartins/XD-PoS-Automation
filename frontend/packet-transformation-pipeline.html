V3 · progressive scrubber

Slide right. Watch the readings telescope outward.

At 0, only the HEX is open — the same 482 bytes, full width. Drag right and the next stage opens up next to it, compressing what came before into a thin column you can still see. By 3 all four stages are visible, with the action on the right occupying the most space. Closest to your literal "horizontal expansion" idea.

V4 · connected pipeline

Draw the lines. Show the flow.

Same four stages, but the relationships between them are explicit: SVG curves connect each field's bytes to its decoded form, to its labelled row, to its place in the action. Each curve is the field's color. The dashes flow gently. Hovering a field dims every other curve so you can follow only that thread.

My hunch: V1 is the strongest as a production candidate (always-on cross-reference is dense without being noisy). V3 best matches the "horizontal expansion" phrase. V4 is the prettiest. V2 is good if you want to commit screen real estate to whichever stage the reader is on. Approach E (the lens) is great for a deep-dive page or an annotated docs spread; not this section.
